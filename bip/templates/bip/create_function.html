    Examples of observable and measurable behaviors:

  • Attention-Seeking: Some behaviors are a way for individuals to gain attention from others. For example, a child might misbehave in a classroom to get the teacher's attention.
  • Escape/Avoidance: Behaviors can occur to escape or avoid a particular situation or task. For instance, a person might fake an illness to avoid going to work or school.
  • Obtaining/Accessing: Behaviors can be a means to obtain or access something desirable. For instance, a person might engage in a certain behavior, like asking politely, to obtain a reward or a desired item.
  • Sensory Stimulation: Sometimes, behaviors occur because they provide some form of sensory stimulation or satisfaction. This could include actions like rocking back and forth or tapping fingers as a way to self-soothe or regulate sensory input.
